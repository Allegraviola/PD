Personality disorders are part of mental illness. They involve consistent
patterns of thinking and behavior that differ from what is typical in your
society. Attitudes and actions are rigid and harmful. They seriously hinder
relationships, productivity and social interaction. They can make it difficult
to face the challenges and stresses of everyday life. 
To be classified as a personality disorder, one's way of thinking, feeling and
behaving deviates from cultural expectations, causes anxiety or dysfunction,
and persists over time. The pattern of experience and behavior usually begins
in adolescence or early adulthood and causes pain or dysfunction. Without
treatment, personality disorders can be long-lasting. 

A mental health provider can diagnose personality disorders. A mental health
professional is a doctor who specializes in diagnosing and treating mental health
problems. In making a diagnosis, the provider considers the person's symptoms,
experiences, and family history. A full medical examination may also be performed
to rule out other possible causes of the symptoms.
